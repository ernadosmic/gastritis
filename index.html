Understanding Gastritis & H. pylori

What is H. pylori?

Helicobacter pylori is a bacteria that can cause inflammation of the stomach lining (gastritis) and ulcers. Proper diet is crucial for healing.

Diet's Role

While antibiotics treat the infection, the right foods can reduce inflammation, promote healing, and prevent symptom flares.

Healing Timeline

Recovery varies, but following dietary guidelines consistently for 4-8 weeks typically shows significant improvement.

Cooking & Lifestyle Tips

Gentle Cooking Methods

  • Bake, boil, steam, or poach instead of frying
  • Use minimal oil when cooking
  • Cook vegetables until soft
  • Remove skins from fruits when possible

Mild Seasonings

  • Use turmeric (anti-inflammatory)
  • Fresh parsley and dill are gentle
  • Small amounts of salt are okay
  • Avoid hot peppers and vinegar

Eating Habits

  • Eat smaller, more frequent meals
  • Chew food thoroughly
  • Don't eat 2-3 hours before bed
  • Stay upright after eating

Lifestyle Changes

  • Quit smoking if you smoke
  • Manage stress through relaxation
  • Get adequate sleep
  • Follow your doctor's medication schedule

Important Medical Notice

This dietary guide is for informational purposes only and should not replace professional medical advice. Always consult with your healthcare provider before making significant dietary changes, especially when dealing with H. pylori infection and gastritis. Individual responses to foods may vary.
